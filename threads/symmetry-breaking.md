# Thread: Symmetry-breaking strategies as a first-class design artifact

## Question

How does Chorognomon represent, accumulate, and *evaluate over time* a library of symmetry-breaking strategies — moves that give a design a starting place when the constraint-set is too thin to determine one on its own?

Adjacent: how do we distinguish *deliberate willful asymmetry* (a modernist design move) from *unexamined arbitrary asymmetry* (someone picked left-justified because they always do)?

## What we know so far

- philetus's principle: *willful asymmetry is the soul of modernism.*
- An important part of getting better at design over time is developing a kit of symmetry-breaking strategies that work as defaults until system constraints develop enough to override them.
- This is *content* the Boot Room should accumulate (specific strategies and how they fared), and also a *principle* the system itself follows (don't force symmetry on the human/agent interfaces just because symmetry feels tidy).

## Why it matters

- Touches the schema for what kinds of "design knowledge" the Boot Room is meant to hold (it's not just "what worked / what didn't" — it includes "what arbitrary choice we made and how it played").
- Touches the philosophy of the GUI/MCP split: leaning *into* the asymmetry is the right move, not papering over it.

## What would resolve it

- Inventory symmetry-breaking strategies philetus uses in practice; classify them.
- Decide where they live in the schema (separate object type? a tag on Equipment? part of campaign retrospectives?).
- Sketch the evaluation loop: how do we tell, over time, whether a given symmetry-breaking strategy has earned its keep?
