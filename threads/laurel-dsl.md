# Thread: Laurel-bestowal DSL + automated retrospection

## Question

What's the right form for the criteria-language a campaign uses to declare a laurel at launch, and what does the bestowal procedure look like? Does it scale from human-judged case-study post-analysis toward something more automated without losing fidelity?

## What we know so far

- README: laurels are *declared* at campaign launch, *bestowed* retrospectively when stated criteria are met. The bestowed record lives in the Boot Room and feeds future coalitions.
- philetus is currently chasing vibes on the laurel roster (the specific deity/epithet pairings) — many were suggested by another Claude in the README-drafting conversation. Open to revision.
- philetus's instinct for the bestowal mechanic: start with case-study style post-analysis (human-led), then tighten the feedback loop with automation.
- An automated bestowal loop is potentially a major win — it's where the whole "did we actually do the thing" question gets answered, and tight loops here probably matter more than tight loops anywhere else in the system.

## Why it matters

- The criteria-language constrains what kinds of campaigns can even be expressed cleanly. Free-form prose is maximally flexible but defeats automation; structured predicates enable automation but might force teams to declare goals in shapes that distort them.
- Bestowal is the system's *truth-generating event* — it's how Boot Room knowledge gets minted from Stoa activity.

## What would resolve it

- Pick 2–3 hypothetical campaigns, write their declared criteria three ways (free-form, structured-predicate, hybrid). Compare.
- Walk a case-study bestowal end-to-end on paper to surface what the human-led version actually looks like. Use that to find the parts that obviously can be automated.
- Decide whether laurels are mutually exclusive per campaign, or whether one campaign can earn several (e.g., Athena Nike *and* Hephaestus Kallitechnes if it was both clever and well-built).
