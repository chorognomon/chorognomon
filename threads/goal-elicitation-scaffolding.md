# Thread: Scaffolding goal-elicitation

## Question

How does Chorognomon scaffold humans (and human/agent teams) into articulating *what kind of future they want* — the high-level goals that are both universal and elusive — well enough that the rest of the system (campaigns, niches, laurels) has something real to grip?

## What we know so far

- philetus has practical experience across the full arc: design-space analysis → goal/requirement elicitation → low-hanging-fruit identification → scheme design → follow-through → evaluation. There are reusable patterns in this work that can probably be encoded.
- One stated product goal: scaffold teams toward an understanding of *what kind of future they will like*. Treated as central, not peripheral.
- The Laurel system already does some scaffolding-by-vocabulary: declaring a campaign as Athena Nike vs. Dionysus Eleuthereus is itself a goal-articulation move.

## Why it matters

If teams can't articulate goals well, every downstream artifact (campaigns, niches, retrospectives, laurel bestowals) is built on sand. This is plausibly the highest-leverage scaffolding the system provides.

## What would resolve it

- Inventory the elicitation patterns philetus has used in practice; pick the ones that translate to a markdown-document substrate.
- Sketch a "goal-elicitation flow" that a Stoa walks a new team through — what prompts, what artifacts get produced, what graduates to the Boot Room.
- Decide whether the laurel roster *is* the goal vocabulary, or whether goals live one level above and laurels are how a campaign declares its mode.
