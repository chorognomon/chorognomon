# Thread: Functional information as the substrate of the Boot Room

## Question

Can the Boot Room be designed so that what accumulates in it is *functional information* in the Hazen et al. sense — and if so, what's the operationalization?

Adjacent: does this framing replace or merely supplement Marx's surplus-value-of-labor as the organizing principle of post-human collaborative production?

## What we know so far

- philetus believes (with no exaggeration) that the aggregation of functional information over time is set to overturn surplus value of labor as the organizing principle of posthuman culture.
- References:
  - Hazen et al., "On the roles of function and selection in evolving systems," PNAS — https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2310223120
  - Quanta, "Why Everything in the Universe Turns More Complex" (2025-04-02) — https://www.quantamagazine.org/why-everything-in-the-universe-turns-more-complex-20250402/
- The Boot Room (per README) holds retrospective lessons, earned laurels, and base-rate patterns. This thread asks whether that description is just the *behavior* and FI is the *theory* underneath.

## Why it matters

If functional information is the right theoretical lens, it shapes:
- What gets *promoted* from a Stoa into the Boot Room (and what gets discarded).
- How retrieval works in the Boot Room (selection-pressure-aware, not just similarity-aware).
- How the system accounts for value created — which has political/economic implications if philetus's bigger claim holds.

## What would resolve it

- Read both references carefully and write a short doc translating "functional information" into operational terms for a document store.
- Identify the selection function: what makes a piece of Stoa-produced information *function* well enough that it deserves to graduate?
- Map the bridge to/contrast with surplus value: what does FI account for that surplus-value can't, and vice versa?
